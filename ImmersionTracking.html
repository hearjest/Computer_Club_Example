Wait what, Japanese?

Yep. I started learning around March~ish of sophomore year. I took it very casually in the beginning. Too casually. My attention span has been reduced to atoms over the years.

I enjoyed being able to read, but what I could read (virtually nothing interesting) and the immense drag it felt to go through a textbook and workbook with my current attention span set me back far.

I did at only one grammar point per day along with the workbook exercises. Yeah, I made progress, albeit at a snail's pace.

Thankfully I kept to the routine over the summer and did my Anki vocab learning/reviews. Now that I have accumulated some understanding of the grammar points (N4 LMAO), I've decided to engage in Japanese content to keep me motivated whilst I study.

I've kept to reading Kino's Journey starting around like two months ago? I didn't get much done because I got caught up in the ususal trap of finding the "perfect" immerison material. Wasted a bunch of time, least I know now.

I've been using a pop-up dictionary to help me define new words, then using the same dictionary to add the word and sentence to Anki. Bonus points if they use Japanese in a way I've never seen before (probably grammar points)

Other than that, its been mostly podcasts from native speakers on regular, daily life stuff.

Light novels on the other hand...make me VERY uncomfortable. Very inconvient considering they bring the best of both worlds from speaking and reading.
Using text extractor, an auto line copier, and auto copy and paster, along with the actual voice lines, I can listen to a native say the line and use the pop-up dictionary if I don't understand anything. Very inconvient.

I don't mind the plot all that much (still irks at me a bit though), its just the characters I have a problem with. I'll leave it at that.
